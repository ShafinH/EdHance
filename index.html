About

Our web application has many features which utilize machine learning to ehance the learning experience.

  • Equation checker and solver, showing your missteps
  • Essay reviewer and grader giving perzonalized feedback
  • Plagiarism checker which can be used by students and teahcers

As high school students, we have had to transition from in person learning to virtual learning, and we know how hard it can be for students to communicate with teachers and get the help they need. Thus, we created EdHance

How our models work

Our equation solver and checker uses OpenCV and a CNN to detect the numbers, mathmatical operations, and exponents. It seperates every line of work with rectangles, and highlights correct steps in green, and missteps in red.

Our essay grader usees many natural language processing features in order to grade the essay on a scale of 0-5 based on organization, work flow, logic, and staying on topic.

Our essay reviewer grades upon organization, flow, and logical sentences. It bases upon staying on point, organization of paragraphs, and logic. We chose these features as it is hard to grade a random essay based on content due as it would then take very long to train our models as well as apply it on the text. We decided to move away from a vocab grader as they are common and we believe vocab is not as important as the flow.

F.A.Q

Frequently Asked Questions

  • What does the essay grader/reviewer look for?

    The essay reviewer looks mostly for organization and flow of the essay. We feel that grading vocab is not as important, and it is very hard to grade based on content becuase there are so many topics in the world. So we decided to review essays based on organization as we believe that is the next most important factor.

  • What type of math problems does the equation checker work on?

    Currently, our equation checker only works on algebriac expressions, however in the future we plan on expanding to trig functions, log functions, and more!
